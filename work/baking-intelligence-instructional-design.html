Goal
Outcome

Develop a product that uses instructional design principles to show students how to bake industry standard cakes

ADDIE Framework

In order to create a system where users feel a sense of achievement when taking our courses we designed the learning curriculum using ADDIE which helped inform user persona from research findings, production of videos learning content and evaluation of learning objectives. There

Analysis Phase

Through doing thorough analysis before implementing. training we were able to save time and resources. The analysis phase helped us understand the users needs and offer a solution that will help solve those needs in a cost effective manner. In order to achieve a wholesome experience we broke down this phase into 4 sub-phases: the development of instructional goals, an instructional analysis, a learner analysis and development of learning.
Instructional Goal
Here we are able to identify the purpose of our instructional study and the desired outcome we require from the learner. We identified that in each module of Baking Intelligence we want to be able train students on tips and techniques to bake excellent cakes which can easily be replicated across different recipes.
Instructional Ananlysis
Here we identified all the steps required to carry out the instructional goal and broke them down into sub-steps in order to identify how the videos profiles will be scripted and what evaluation will be required
Learner Analysis
We used this sub-phase to discover what learners already know in order to inform how information is structured within the videos. In order to do this we conducted research with a sample size of Sweetheart by Cassata followers who have demonstrated interest in learning how to bake cakes. We received over 120 survey responses, had one physical focus group and one remote focus group and interviewed 24 of our most frequent users. From this research we were able to develop and user persona for our learner
Learning Objective
At the end of each course students must be able to:
‍
1. Describe how to make a baked product from scratch
2. Demonstrate how to prepare ingredients needed for baking
3. Show how to mix dry and wet ingredients of a recipe correctly
4. Explain at what temperature the cake has to be baked and for how long

Design Phase

In this phase the focus was designing the learning objectives, content, subject matter analysis, exercise, lesson planning, assessment instruments used and media selection
Assessment design
Here we used information on performance objectives gathered from the analysis phase to inform the design of the assessment. The questions were structured in non ambiguous grammar to accomodate the literacy levels of some students. We resulted to using plain text format for baking assignment submission and photo uploads for finished product demonstration
Course Format Design
The courses were delivered through video tutorials designed using adobe premiere pro. The scripting and structure of each course being informed by the instructional analysis phase in order to ensure the the video content is suited for our user persona. The course curriculum is broken down into 3 sections namely introduction overview, basics of baking, recipes and assignments.
User Feedback Design
The feedback mechanism we used to determine if the participants are able to digest the lessons is an interactive comment section where users can share their thoughts on the material. This will drive a sense of community among users and will help us get feedback from our sessions.

Development

Development phase involved creating content of learning outcomes. The major media used here were books and video content
Video Contents

Here I had to create the design language and presets for recurring clips using Adobe Premiere Pro. The video content was the hosted on a private non-downloadable video servers to ensure privacy
Ebooks

Implementation

The site was developed in HTML, CSS with PHP being used as the server side scripting language. In order to ensure that the website is editable by non technical members we employed the use of Wordpress as a CMS for the platform

Evaluation

We performed formative evaluation at every step of the process. Evaluation is done throughout the implementation phase with the aid of the instructor and the students. After implementation of a course or program is over, a summative evaluation is done for instructional improvement. Throughout the evaluation phase we were able to ascertain whether problems relevant to the baking program are solved, and whether the desired objectives are met through quizes and assignments. During evaluation we were able to identify the usability of the website through heatmap data gotten from Hotjar and through an iterative and incremental process we were able to solve some usability issues.
